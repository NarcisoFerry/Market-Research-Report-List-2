<p><p>The food thickening agents market comprises three main types: hydrocolloids, proteins, and starches. Hydrocolloids, like gelatin and pectin, enhance texture and stability in various food products. Proteins, such as casein and whey, contribute to thickening by emulsifying and creating a gel-like consistency. Starches, derived from sources like corn and potato, thicken mixtures through gelatinization when heated. Each type serves distinct functional roles in food formulation, improving mouthfeel and overall quality in sauces, soups, and desserts.</p></p>
