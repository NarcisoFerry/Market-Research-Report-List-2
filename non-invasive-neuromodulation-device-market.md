<p><p>The non-invasive neuromodulation device market includes various technologies for modulating brain activity. Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) uses constant low electrical current to alter neuronal excitability. Transcranial Alternating Current Stimulation (tACS) employs alternating currents to synchronize neural rhythms. Transcranial Pulsed Current Stimulation (tPCS) delivers brief pulses to influence neural activity. Transcranial Random Noise Stimulation (tRNS) uses random noise signals to enhance cognitive functions. Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT), while traditionally invasive, is utilized in newer, non-invasive forms for mood disorders. Other devices may include variations or combinations of these methods.</p></p>
