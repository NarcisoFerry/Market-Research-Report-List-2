<p><p>The Brain Machine Interfaces (BMI) market is divided into two main types: invasive and non-invasive. Invasive BMIs involve surgical implantation of devices directly into the brain, offering high precision and control for medical applications like restoring movement in paralyzed patients. Non-invasive BMIs, on the other hand, utilize external sensors to detect brain activity without surgery, making them safer and more accessible for applications such as gaming, rehabilitation, and mental health monitoring. Both markets are rapidly evolving, addressing diverse needs.</p></p>
